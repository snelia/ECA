Abstract


As the popularity rises for global and domestic wellness retreats as tourist destinations, their predominantly Western audience invites a particularly American offshoot of Orientalism that exoticizes and commodifies Eastern practices into “mindfulness” rather than acknowledging their origins of medicine and religion. This paper examines the catalog of Miraval Berkshires as a demonstration of Eastern practices in wellness retreats. More than half of the specialists employ names of Eastern techniques to establish credibility for the retreat by invoking tradition and to exoticize the practices using the word “ancient.” This trend confirms the major role the wellness industry has in representing Eastern practices and illustrates the importance of challenging these misnomers of traditions.
